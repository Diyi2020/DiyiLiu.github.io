Welcome! I'm a Postdoctoral Researcher at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, working with Bert de Jong, Chao Yang, and Lin Lin.

I am passionate about **Computational and Applied Mathematics**, especially in developing numerical methods and mathematical tools for applications in **Condensed Matter Physics**, **Quantum Technology**, and **Mechanics**. My main mathematical interests lie in **numerical analysis**, **applied analysis**, and **mathematical physics**, particularly in the context of **quantum materials science and quantum information**.

## Research Overview

My research focuses on improving numerical methods and mathematical tools motivated by applications in condensed matter physics and quantum technology. I work on problems in:
- **Numerical analysis and applied analysis**
- **Quantum many-body systems**: quantum materials and quantum devices  
- **Mathematical physics and quantum information**

### A Broader Perspective on Numerical Analysis for Quantum Computing

Numerical analysis has traditionally focused on analyzing logical complexity under error-tolerance requirements. However, in quantum computing, the way a logical gate is physically implemented can strongly affect overall algorithm performance. This suggests a broader notion of **numerical analysis for quantum computation**—one that analyzes physical complexity subject to logical error tolerance.
